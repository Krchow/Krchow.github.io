Soul-Searching or the Simulation to Evaluate the First Digital Brain

Kevin Ronnie Chow is an architectural designer and big technopositivist with the blood of Silicon Valley in his veins. He has a Bachelor of Architecture from the Irwin S. Chanin School of Architecture of the Cooper Union and will have a Master of Architecture II from Princeton University’s School of Architecture by May 2024 if all goes well. He is a corecipient of the 2021 Benjamin Menschel Fellowship for fully-funded research toward an exhibition titled “Pastoral Silicon Valley: A Landscape of Quiet Crisis,” a critical revisiting of his hometown. He was named a Future100 Architecture Undergraduate by Metropolis Magazine as one of the “top graduating architecture and interior design students in the United States and Canada.” He is a recipient of the SOM Foundation’s 2021 Robert L. Wesley Award, which awarded $5000 to pursue research for a film.

Current interests: DSMP, Twitch, AI alignment, transhumanism
